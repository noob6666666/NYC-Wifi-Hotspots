About the data provider

This website uses data from NYC Open Data, the City of New York's public data portal. The Wi-Fi hotspot records are published by the Office of Technology and Innovation, formerly known as the Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications. OTI supports city technology services and makes datasets available so residents, students, and researchers can understand how public resources are distributed across the five boroughs.

About the dataset

We use the NYC Wi-Fi Hotspot Locations dataset, stored on this site as mvc.json. Each record describes a public Wi-Fi location and includes fields such as provider, hotspot name, borough, city, zip code, Wi-Fi type , SSID, and latitude/longitude coordinates. The data covers parks, public spaces, and other city-supported access points.

Why this data matters

Reliable internet access is important for schoolwork, job searches, healthcare, and everyday life. Mapping where free public Wi-Fi is available helps show which neighborhoods are well served and where gaps may remain. This site makes the open dataset easier to explore by listing hotspot details and offering simple filters, so users do not need to download and search the raw file on their own.

What you will find on this site

Info page: Browse individual hotspot records shown as cards (provider, city, location name, zip, and type). Use dropdown filters to search by borough, or combine borough and provider to narrow results.

Analysis page: View charts that summarize patterns in the data, including line, bar, pie, and doughnut visualizations (for example, hotspots by borough or by provider).
